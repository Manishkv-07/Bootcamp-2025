Essay on 15 August

15 August – Independence Day of India

1. Independence Day is one of the most significant national festivals of India. It is celebrated every year on 15th August to honor the day in 1947 when India gained freedom from British rule. This day reminds us of the struggle, sacrifices, and bravery of thousands of freedom fighters who fought for the independence of our country.

2.Before 1947, India was under British control for nearly 200 years. During this time, Indians were treated unfairly and had no control over their own country. Many great leaders like Mahatma Gandhi, Subhas Chandra Bose, Bhagat Singh, Jawaharlal Nehru, and Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel played important roles in the freedom movement. They inspired people to fight peacefully or bravely against British rule and finally helped India become a free nation.

3. Every year on 15th August, the Prime Minister of India hoists the national flag at the Red Fort in Delhi and gives a speech to the nation. This event is watched by people all over the country. The Indian Army, Navy, and Air Force also take part in the celebration by performing parades and saluting the national flag. In schools and colleges, students participate in flag hoisting ceremonies, cultural programs, patriotic songs, speeches, and skits. People decorate their homes, streets, and vehicles with the colors of the Indian flag – saffron, white, and green

4.Independence Day is not just a celebration, but a reminder that we should always value and protect our freedom. It is our duty as citizens to be honest, responsible, and contribute to the development of our country. We must stay united and work for peace and progress. Let us always respect our nation and salute the heroes who made India free.

Jai Hind!
